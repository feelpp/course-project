== Shared Context

This project is designed for M1 CSMI Semester 2 students in 2026.
It uses ICU sepsis data (PhysioNet challenge format) and focuses on supervised prediction in a clinically interpretable setting.
The expected workload is 60 to 80 hours (maximum 100 hours) between March 10, 2026 and May 30, 2026 (oral defense).

== Student-Specific Scope

- Assigned student(s): _to be filled_
- Specific scientific question: How early can sepsis be predicted while maintaining acceptable precision, recall, and calibration?
- Specific constraints: Compare at least one interpretable model and one higher-capacity model with identical train/test protocol.

== Objectives

- Define a prediction target and clinically relevant prediction horizon (for example 4 to 6 hours before onset).
- Train and compare multiple models (for example logistic regression, random forest, gradient boosting).
- Evaluate discrimination and calibration (AUROC, AUPRC, calibration curve, confusion matrix by threshold).
- Propose an alert thresholding strategy and discuss expected false-alert burden.

== Work Plan and Time Budget

- Phase 1 (March 10 to March 23): data familiarization, split protocol, baseline setup (10 to 12h).
- Phase 2 (March 24 to April 20): feature engineering and model training iterations (24 to 30h).
- Phase 3 (April 21 to May 11): evaluation, calibration, error analysis, subgroup checks (20 to 24h).
- Phase 4 (May 12 to May 24): final report, model card, slides, rehearsal (10 to 14h).
- Oral defense: May 30, 2026.
- Total target: 60 to 80h (up to 100h with optional advanced experiments).

== Expected Deliverables

- Reproducible training/evaluation pipeline with clear experiment tracking.
- Comparative results table and plots (ROC, PR, calibration, threshold trade-offs).
- Model card including assumptions, limitations, and potential bias risks.
- Reproducibility guide (`README` with environment and run instructions).
- Final written report and oral presentation slides.

== Optional Extension (if time allows)

- External-style validation protocol on a held-out cohort segment.
- Drift sensitivity check by simulating temporal split shifts.
